Correlation with Heatmap¶

A correlation heatmap uses colored cells, typically in a monochromatic scale, to show a 2D correlation matrix(table) between two discrete dimensions or event types. It is very important in Feature Section

In [4]:
df.corr()
Out[4]:
total_bill tip size
total_bill 1.000000 0.675734 0.598315
tip 0.675734 1.000000 0.489299
size 0.598315 0.489299 1.000000
In [5]:
sns.heatmap(df.corr())
Out[5]:
<matplotlib.axes._subplots.AxesSubplot at 0x2a3abb83d08>

JoinPlot¶

A join plot allows to study the relationship between 2 numeric variables.The central chart display their correlation. It is usually a scatterplot, a hexbin plot, a 2D histogram or a 2D density plot

In [6]:
sns.jointplot(x='tip',y='total_bill',data=df,kind='hex')
Out[6]:
<seaborn.axisgrid.JointGrid at 0x2a3abc6be48>

PairPlot¶

A "pair plot" also known as scatterplot, in which one variable in the same data rows matched with another variable's.
